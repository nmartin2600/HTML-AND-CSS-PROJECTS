Impact

Grace Hopper helped transform computers from specialized machines programmed in raw machine code into systems that could use human-readable programming languages. Her work on early compilers and the development of COBOL made software development faster, more accessible, and practical for large-scale business and government use. She also helped establish many foundational ideas in modern computer programming and became a major public advocate for computer science education and innovation.
